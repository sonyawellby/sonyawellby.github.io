Unknown writer

Sonya Joan

Why, hello there. Thank you for joining me. I mean it. I am pleasurably warmed to have you here. (My toes are wriggling in my slippers.)

So, what can I say about me? I'm just like you, really, in that I have a dreamy brain and an unquenched thirst for stories. In trivial things we differ, I am sure - but those details are not relevant. However, to titillate the host of "About" pages drifting above in that omnipresent cloud, I should provide some thoroughly unimportant, concrete details.

I am an unknown writer. This is rather unilluminating, so here are some details for which I am known. I live in Canberra (Australia), although I was born in Melbourne and chrysalised in Hobart (Tasmania). I am a full-time university student at the Australian National University, where I am completing my honours thesis in environmental science. I like green tea, jazz, and foggy mornings. The writers I admire most are (in no order of greatness or literariness): Haruki Murakami, Sylvia Plath, Philip Pullman, George Orwell, and John Keats.
